Mobile App Case Study

"Stress less at your desk"

Project Summary

Over the course of five weeks, my partner and I were tasked with creating a mobile app which came to life as Releaf, a stress-relieving app made to provide employees with a way to relax, reset, and prioritize their mental health throughout a long work day.

My Responsibilities

Throughout the course of this project, I played the parts of both user researcher as well as designer, working collaboratively with my partner to execute a cohesive, interactive design.

The Problem

Users who experience work-induced stress need a way to identify their feelings, prioritize their well-being and find relief from work-related anxiety, helping them reduce and manage their stress levels so that at the end of the day, they are more relaxed and able to leave work at the office.

The Solution

Releaf offers employees easy stress identification tools, coupled with suggested relief techniques based on user preferences. Releaf promotes office well being, company bonding, and respect for colleague availability, all while encouraging employees to leave work at the office once finished for the day. Keep up moral by caring for employee mental health.

The Process

USER RESEARCH

Interviews

Our team conducted a series of four virtual interviews which were all transcribed using a transcription service. Each interviewee was asked a series of questions regarding the ways in which they deal with difficult times and the tools they might use to express their emotions - talking with others, physical activity, creative outlets, etc.

Surveys

In addition to a number of user interviews, we conducted a survey using Google Forms for more quantitative results. We received over 10 seperate responses which we were able to record and use during affinitization.

Affinitization

During the affinitization process, our team was able to successfully group and categorize specific information pertaining to stress and how our interviewees view stress and coping mechanisms they use.
